Flower Friends

Branding, web presence, and social media campaigns for a small creative jewelry business

Overview

Flower Friends is a whimsical, nature-inspired resin charm jewelry brand that I independently launched and operated. I led the end-to-end development of the business including brand identity, product design, Shopify website creation, product photography, and digital marketing.

Problem

The handmade charm jewelry market often prioritizes either aesthetic uniqueness (intricate clay designs) or production efficiency (clear resin pieces), but rarely both.

Clay-based charms often require individual hand-sculpting, which can limit scalability and lead to product inconsistencies, while resin designs frequently lack playful character-driven forms.

How might I create whimsical animal and nature-themed charms that are visually distinctive, lightweight, and efficient to produce at scale?

Research & Inspiration

Flower Friends was designed for ages 5+ with a focus on playful, nature-inspired charm jewelry. The goal was to create accessories that felt joyful and imaginative while standing out from traditional geometric resin pieces or labor-intensive clay charms.

The visual direction was inspired by kawaii aesthetics, pastel palettes, and summer nature imagery - grass, sunshine, flowers, and open skies. The emotional tone centers on carefree outdoor play and lighthearted creativity.

Because many purchases were made by parents or family members, durability, safety, and price accessibility were key considerations alongside visual appeal. Due to the small size of the charms, the recommended age range was set at 5+, informed by safety research indicating that younger children face a higher risk of choking hazards.

I began by sketching concepts and sculpting clay prototypes of the designs I wanted to turn into charms. Due to limitations in my clay-forming skills, the early versions required significant refinement. After creating molds and casting resin copies, I realized that hand-painting each charm was time-intensive and difficult to scale. I also struggled to find a primer and sealant that adhered properly to resin and prevented wear over time.

To improve efficiency and durability, I transitioned to silicone molds and incorporated resin dye directly into the casting process. This eliminated the need for extensive hand-painting, streamlined production, and resulted in a more professional and consistent final product.

Brand Identity Development

The brand identity was built around softness, warmth, and whimsy.

Logo & Typography

The primary logo uses the rounded typeface Bestime to reinforce a playful, child-friendly tone. A lighter sans-serif system font was used on the website to balance personality with readability and usability.

Color Palette

The palette draws from natural summer elements — grassy greens, warm yellow, and sky blues — creating a cheerful yet gentle visual system that feels bright without being overwhelming.

Together, these elements establish a cohesive, whimsical brand that translates consistently across products and digital platforms.

Website Design (UX)

Site Goals

The website was designed to support clear product browsing, drive purchases, and encourage email sign-ups.

A key feature was the interactive “Design-a-Friend” customization page, allowing users to select colors, accessories, and product formats (earrings, necklace, keychain), adding a playful, engaging element to the shopping experience.

Standard Purchase Flow

Home → Collection → Product Page → Add to Cart → Checkout

Customization Flow

Home → Design-a-Friend → Customize → Add to Cart → Checkout

Navigation was intentionally shallow to minimize friction and keep the path to purchase simple.

Product Photography & Craft

Product visuals played a significant role in communicating quality and brand tone.

Lifestyle photography was shot outdoors in natural light (grass, leaves, pond settings) to reinforce the summery, nature-inspired identity of the brand. Soft lighting and organic backdrops aligned with the playful, backyard-inspired emotional tone.

Marketing & Ads

Social media content included short-form reels, static posts, and lifestyle product photography shot outdoors in natural light to reinforce the brand’s summery, nature-inspired tone.

Variables Tested

  • Pastel, character-focused teddy bear charms vs. beachy, seashell-themed jewelry
  • Close-up product shots vs. styled outdoor lifestyle imagery
  • Organic posting vs. paid Meta ads

Audience Experiments & Insights

Instagram generated stronger engagement compared to Facebook and TikTok, particularly within the small business community. While paid ads produced some link clicks, organic Instagram content led to higher visibility and interaction.

Reflection

Flower Friends validated the importance of cohesive branding and community engagement. Within the first month, the Instagram account grew to 100+ followers, and early marketing efforts generated over $200 in sales through personal networks.

Participating in a local holiday market led to my first out-of-network sales, reinforcing the importance of in-person visibility and brand storytelling.

What I Would Improve

  • Explore more environmentally conscious materials to better align with the nature-driven brand identity
  • Refine production methods (e.g., two-sided molds) to improve product dimensionality and perceived quality
  • Adjust website tone to balance whimsy for young users with credibility for purchasing decision-makers

Key Takeaways

This project strengthened my understanding of how branding, product design, and UX intersect. I learned that aesthetic cohesion builds trust, interactive features increase engagement, and small refinements in material or presentation can significantly impact perceived value.
