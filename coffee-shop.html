Studio Brew Coffee App

Mobile Ordering Experience Design

Overview

Studio Brew was a pop-up coffee shop concept created to serve students near McNeal Hall, focusing on quick, convenient, and enjoyable coffee experiences.

Project Goal: Design a mobile app and in-person service flow to streamline ordering, improve campus coffee accessibility, and provide a cozy, student-focused environment.

Target Audience:
Students and faculty seeking fast, affordable, and quality coffee options on campus.

My Role:
Digital/web team - app wireframing, UX flow, and ordering system design.

Tools Used:
Figma (wireframes and interactive prototype), Miro (research synthesis), Canva (visual polish), in-class testing and observation.

Research

User Interviews & Surveys

  • Students want quick, efficient ordering options to save time between classes.
  • Variety of drink options is valued, including hot/cold and non-coffee beverages.
  • Students prioritize energy and affordability.
  • Loyalty or rewards tracking increases repeat usage.

Competitive & Service Safari Analysis

  • Caribou Coffee and Starbucks apps provide mobile ordering but lack campus-specific location filtering.
  • Physical spaces benefit from warm, inviting atmospheres with adequate seating and study space.
  • Common pain points: confusing QR codes, inconsistent hours, unclear pickup locations.

Problem Statement

Busy students need a fast and convenient way to order coffee and pick it up on campus without waiting in line or navigating confusing menus.

User Persona

Name: Alex, 20, undergraduate student

Goals: Get coffee quickly between classes, customize drinks, earn loyalty rewards.

Frustrations: Long lines, unclear menus, inconsistent hours, and lack of nearby coffee options.

User Flow

Designed to minimize steps and cognitive load:

  • Open App → Browse Menu → Customize Drink → Add to Cart → Checkout → Pickup

The flow ensures users can quickly find their desired drink, customize options, and pick up efficiently. Pickup location visibility and order status reduce uncertainty.

Wireframes

Low-Fidelity

Initial sketches focused on essential components: menu categories, drink customization, and quick checkout. Layout emphasized minimal scrolling and easy tap targets.

Mid-Fidelity

Added branding elements (vintage, student-focused style), clearer icons, and structured categories. Buttons and navigation were designed for thumb reach and rapid ordering.

Final Prototype

Navigation & Accessibility: Bottom sticky navigation for easy access, large category icons, and high-contrast typography to support quick scanning and accessibility.

Visual Hierarchy: Primary actions (add to cart, checkout) emphasized with color; menu categories organized for fast decision-making.

Key Design Decisions

  • Sticky bottom navigation for thumb-friendly reach.
  • Large, clear category icons for drink selection.
  • Minimal checkout steps to reduce friction.
  • Order status visible to reduce wait anxiety.

Reflection

What Worked

  • Clear UX flow made ordering intuitive for students in a time-constrained environment.
  • Prototype integrated feedback from in-class pop-up test effectively.
  • Branding and visual hierarchy reinforced the cozy, student-friendly Studio Brew identity.

What I’d Refine

  • Include more robust loyalty and rewards tracking for repeated visits.
  • Expand accessibility features, like alternative text for images and screen reader navigation.
  • Test with larger, diverse student populations for usability insights.
